Frequently Asked Questions

  • Zakat is an obligatory annual alms tax on wealth prescribed for Muslims to help the poor and needy.

  • Muslims who have reached maturity and possess wealth exceeding the nisab are required to pay Zakat.

  • The nisab is determined as the minimum amount of wealth upon which Zakat is due. For gold, it is 85 grams; for silver, it is 595 grams.

  • The rate of Zakat is typically 2.5% of the wealth exceeding the nisab.

  • Zakat is due on money, gold/silver items, commercial goods, agricultural products, livestock, and rights to extract natural resources.

  • Zakat can be received by the poor and needy, those who collect and distribute Zakat, new converts to Islam, slaves seeking freedom, debtors, for the cause of Allah, and travelers in need.

  • Yes, Zakat can be paid in installments, but the full amount must be paid within one year.

  • Yes, Zakat can be paid in advance if there is confidence that the wealth will remain above the nisab until the end of the year.

  • It is recommended to consult a scholar in Islamic law or use Zakat calculators available on many Islamic websites.

  • One must estimate the unpaid Zakat for all the previous years and pay it as soon as possible.
